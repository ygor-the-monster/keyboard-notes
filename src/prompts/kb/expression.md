## Dynamics
Dynamics are the loudness of the music, marked with Italian abbreviations from soft to loud: ppp (very very soft), pp (pianissimo, very soft), p (piano, soft), mp (mezzo-piano, medium soft), mf (mezzo-forte, medium loud), f (forte, loud), ff (fortissimo, very loud), fff (very very loud). They are relative, not fixed volumes — mf in a lullaby is quieter than mf in a march.

## Changing dynamics
A crescendo (cresc., or an opening hairpin) means gradually get louder; a diminuendo or decrescendo (dim., decresc., or a closing hairpin) means gradually get softer. A sudden strong accent on one note is sforzando (sfz). These shaping marks are what turn correct notes into expressive phrasing.

## Articulation
Articulation tells you how to attack and release notes:
- Staccato (a dot above the note): short and detached.
- Legato (a slur connecting notes): smooth and connected, no gap between notes.
- Accent (a > mark): emphasize, play that note stronger.
- Tenuto (a short line): hold the note for its full value, slightly stressed.
- Marcato (a ^ mark): strongly accented and somewhat detached.
- Fermata (a "bird's-eye" ⌐ over the note): hold longer than written, at the performer's discretion.

## Ornaments
Ornaments are decorative notes added to a main note:
- Trill (tr): rapidly alternate the written note with the note above.
- Mordent: a quick single alternation with the note above (upper) or below (lower).
- Turn: a four-note figure circling the main note — above, the note, below, the note.
- Grace note (acciaccatura/appoggiatura): a small quick note played just before the main note, "crushed in" or leaning on the beat.

## Common expression terms
Italian terms shape mood and motion: dolce (sweetly), cantabile (in a singing style), espressivo (expressively), legato (smoothly), marcato (marked, accented), rubato (with flexible, "robbed" timing), ritardando/rit. (gradually slowing), accelerando/accel. (gradually speeding up), a tempo (return to the original speed), fine (the end), D.C. al fine (repeat from the top to "fine").
